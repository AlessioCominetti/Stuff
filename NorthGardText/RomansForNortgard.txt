ROMANS
-zones have no building limit: player can build as many buildings
as space allows. Can build multiple type of the same building
in the same zone, but only 1 defense tower per zone.
-Start with no settlers but with +200 W. Houses
can be upgraded using prestige. Houses provide citizens who
will automatically work to collect resources from buildings.
-Need storehouses to colonize. Each storehouse can host
2 captivus, 3 when upgraded and store 400 resources, 800 when upgraded
-Citizens don't build. Captivus are the only units who can
build. They don't count towards population space and consume -60% F.
Slaves can be ordered to build individual buildings
or to work together to speed up the construction of one.
-Increasing a house status also increase work rate but 
also increase citizens demands. Each citizen needs resources and
services to be happy. Unhappy citizens will revolt and start
burning down buildings. Make sure to have at least more than 50% happy
citizens.
-Houses gain status with prestige. Prestige can be increased by
special buildings. Each building increase prestigie of
a zone, but some are more powerful.
-Doesn't have access to lore and techs.
-New resources: clothes and wine. 2 F <> 1 cloth     2 F <> 1 wine
-Miners work slower but deposits last 3 times more
-Always available a trade route with Rome to buy 1 S for 10 F
-Citizen classes:
plebean: 40% work rate, need F,W and water   (-1.2F -0.1W)
low class: 60% work rate, need F,W,water and clothes (+  -0.2 clothes)
middle class: 80% work rate, need F,W,water,clothes and wine in taverns (+ -0.2 wine)
upper class: 100% work rate, need previous +teather and baths
-Can train legatus(warchief) at basilica(town hall)
-Basilica upgrades automatically for every 10 new houses you have
Upgraded basilica bring new benefits
-Vigiles are the only units that can extinguish fires
-Citizens and slaves respawn when killed over a period of 6/3 months
-Soldiers are trained from barracks and don't count towards
civilian unit count
